Sentury

A game inspired by 2015 VRGJ Hackathon hosted by Archiact Interactive. In a two man team, we created a futuristic time traveling first person tower defense game. In the not so distant future, we discovered a way to time travel. However due to the state of future, many proposed to change the past to change the future, they were known as the “Shifters”. Those who opposed that notion were called the “Senturies”. To prevent the “Shifters” from changing history, the “Senturies” set up bases at historically important places. The “Senturies” send spotters who uses time traveling VR machine to spot enemies who try to invade them. Prevent them from invading your base and taking over the generator. That is your job.

Responsibilities
  • Implemented all of the features in the game.
  • Optimized audio files that lagged the game.
  • Implemented and voiced the tutorial scene to guide new players.
  • Created simple texture skins to enrich player experience.
  • Designed gameplay that allowed engaging experience for players.
  • We were asked to participate in CVR2016 to showcase our game at a booth.
Future Implementations
  • Implement subtitles for tutorial.
  • Implement campaign mode.

Car Pool Masters

A team created spontaneously for the nwHackathon 2015. Car Pool Master is a web app that greatly improves the process of matching a driver with a rider. With integration with the Mojio API, a driver just has to register with their id and phone number. A rider enters simple information such as their origin (we can also use their current location with the gps functionality), destination, phone number, and price they are willing to pay. We implemented functionality to search for the the top 5 drivers and send them a text message immediately with the information the rider entered. All interested drivers can communicate the rider and set up an agreement.

Responsibilities
  • Implemented the Mojio API with the web application to parse data coming from and to the Mojio device.
  • Assisted in implmenting Twilio API that allowed the application to send text messages to different users.
